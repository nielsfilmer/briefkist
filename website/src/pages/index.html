What we protect against — and what we don't

Privacy tools should say both. The short version is here; the full threat model is on the security page.

protected against
Cloud providers reading, indexing or training on your documents — there is no cloud in the path.
A network observer on the way to your server — every request is token-authenticated, and traffic runs over TLS (hosted) or stays on your own network or encrypted overlay (self-host).
On hosted: anyone with the server's disks, including us — stored data is ciphertext under a key only you hold.
Losing the paper — the scan, summary and searchable text outlive the original.
not protected against
Malware on your own phone or computer — it sees what you see.
Someone who has your passphrase — it is the key; treat it like one.
Physical access to your own unlocked server at home — that machine is yours to secure.
A lost passphrase — we cannot reset it, and that is the point. Losing it loses the archive.
