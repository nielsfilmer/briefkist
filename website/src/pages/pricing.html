The trade is convenience against control, not features against features. Self-hosting keeps every byte on hardware you own and asks you to do your own updates and backups. Hosted hands us the operations while the key to your documents stays with you — we store ciphertext and can't read your archive at rest. If you're comfortable running a Docker command, self-host; if you'd rather never think about it, we'll run the server.

Questions people actually ask

How does billing work?

Monthly or annual, prices include VAT. Card, SEPA direct debit or iDEAL. Annual billing is simply cheaper — €12 instead of €15, €19 instead of €24 — no other difference. Invoices land in your account dashboard.

Can I cancel?

Any time, self-serve, from the account page — no email, no call. Your server runs until the end of the paid period; export stays available throughout, and you choose when the instance is deleted.

What happens if I lose my passphrase?

We cannot reset it — we never have it, which is the whole design. A lost passphrase means the archive is unrecoverable. At signup we ask you to write it down or download a recovery sheet; please actually do that.

Can I move between tiers, or leave for self-hosting?

Hosted and dedicated migrate in place with a click. Leaving is a full export from the dashboard, then an import on your own server — the same software, so nothing is lost in translation.

What if the company stops?

The entire server is AGPL-3.0 and public. You would export your archive, run the same software on your own machine, and carry on. Hosting is a convenience on top of the open source, not a hostage situation.
