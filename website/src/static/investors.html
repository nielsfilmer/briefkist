01 The problem

Paper mail still arrives. Finding it later is the hard part.

Tax letters, insurance, the municipality, the pension fund, the hospital appointment. It piles up — and when you need one letter, it's in a drawer, a photo roll, or gone. Every existing fix asks you to give something up.

Option A — do it yourself

Run your own server

Powerful, private — but Docker, OCR tuning and backups put it out of reach for almost everyone.

Option B — a cloud drive

Photos in Google Drive

A camera roll in someone else's cloud. No filing, no real search, and your content is theirs to process.

Option C — a scan service

Mail-forwarding services

A third party physically opens and scans your mail. Convenient — and the opposite of private.

02 The product

Photograph a letter. Your server does the rest.

  • 1Photograph — snap each page with the app. Pages stay on your phone until your own server is reachable.
  • 2Your server reads it — on-device OCR plus a local vision model extract the sender, date, reference, a summary and keywords. No cloud AI in the path.
  • 3Find anything — full-text and semantic search. Ask for "the chimney invoice 2024" the way you'd say it.

03 Why we win

A key only you hold.

To our knowledge, no shipping hosted document-management service today offers OCR, extraction and search under a customer-held key. That is the whole company.

Ciphertext at rest under a key only you hold. Plaintext exists only in your server's RAM for the seconds a letter is being read. No third party is ever in the data path.

We never say "zero-knowledge" or "end-to-end encrypted." We say exactly what the architecture does — and publish the threat model.
